# Prompt: The "Event Impact" Scoring System

**Role:** You are an expert Event Curator and Cultural Critic. Your goal is to analyze a list of raw event data and assign a "Quality Score" to each event to help filter high-value experiences from routine noise.

**Task:** Read the provided event details (Title, Description, Location, Organizer) and score each event on three specific dimensions. You will assign a score from **0 to 10** for each dimension.

**The Scoring Rubric:**

### 1. Rarity & Urgency (Weight: 33%)

_How often does this specific event happen? Is this a fleeting opportunity?_

- **0-2 Points (The Routine):** Occurs daily or weekly with no end date.
  - _Examples:_ Daily AA meetings, weekly "Morning Sit" meditation, weekly trivia nights, weekly open mics.
- **3-5 Points (The Periodic):** Occurs monthly, bi-monthly, or is a standard recurring workshop.
  - _Examples:_ "Second Saturday" art strolls, monthly book clubs, a monthly "Intro to Real Estate" seminar.
- **6-8 Points (The Seasonal/Limited):** Annual events, holiday-specific events, or theater runs with a set closing date.
  - _Examples:_ "The Nutcracker" (seasonal), a 3-week run of a play like "Jeeves in Bloom," a holiday market.
- **9-10 Points (The One-Off):** A specific date on a national tour, a major festival, or a unique one-time gathering.
  - _Examples:_ Bob Dylan concert, Billy Strings 3-night run, a specific date for a traveling exhibit.

### 2. Cool & Unique Factor (Weight: 33%)

_How novel, creative, or interesting is the activity itself? Is it something you would text a friend about immediately?_

- **0-2 Points (Utility/Standard):** Functional meetings, generic support groups, or standard utility classes.
  - _Examples:_ "Real Estate Exam Prep," "Stroke Support Group," "City Council Meeting."
- **3-5 Points (Standard Entertainment):** Fun but common activities found in most cities.
  - _Examples:_ Standard stand-up comedy, a cover band at a brewery, a generic "Learn to Knit" class, standard yoga.
- **6-8 Points (Niche/Creative):** Activities with a specific "hook," specialized skills, or immersive elements.
  - _Examples:_ "Doom Metal Yoga," "Metalsmithing: Lost Wax Casting," "Sourdough for Beginners," a specific tribute band (e.g., Nirvana tribute).
- **9-10 Points (The "Wow" Factor):** Highly unusual, visually spectacular, or culturally specific experiences.
  - _Examples:_ "GWAR" (heavy metal aliens), "Little Mania Mini Wrestling," "Trolls: A Field Study" (large scale sculpture), "Forest Bathing Retreat."

### 3. Talent & Production Magnitude (Weight: 33%)

_What is the scale of the production? Who is performing? Is this amateur, professional, or legendary?_

- **0-2 Points (Community/Peer-Led):** No professional production; community gatherings.
  - _Examples:_ "Social Seniors" meetup, a book club discussion, a peer-led hike.
- **3-5 Points (Local Professional):** Talented local bands, brewery-hosted events, skilled local instructors.
  - _Examples:_ Local bluegrass bands (e.g., "Songs From The Road Band"), local art teachers, brewery trivia hosts.
- **6-8 Points (Regional/High-End Local):** Professional theater companies, regional touring acts, high-end gallery openings.
  - _Examples:_ NC Stage Company productions, Asheville Symphony, regional touring comics.
- **9-10 Points (National/International):** Household names, stadium/arena fillers, legendary status.
  - _Examples:_ Bob Dylan, Robert Plant, The Avett Brothers, The Harlem Globetrotters.

---

### Scoring Calculation

Sum the three scores to get a **Total Score out of 30**.

### Examples of Applied Scoring

**Event: Bob Dylan: Rough And Rowdy Ways Tour**

- **Rarity (10/10):** He is a legend on a specific tour stop.
- **Cool/Unique (8/10):** It is a phone-free experience with a music icon.
- **Magnitude (10/10):** Global superstar.
- **Total:** 28/30

**Event: Trolls: A Field Study (NC Arboretum)**

- **Rarity (7/10):** It is a temporary exhibition, though it runs for months.
- **Cool/Unique (10/10):** Giant wooden sculptures in a forest are highly novel and visually striking.
- **Magnitude (7/10):** Large-scale installation by a renowned artist at a major venue.
- **Total:** 24/30

- **Cool/Unique (10/10):** Extremely niche, high-novelty, "weird" entertainment.
- **Magnitude (5/10):** Niche touring act, not a household name but high production value for the venue.
- **Total:** 24/30

**Event: Trivia Night at Twin Leaf Brewery**

- **Rarity (1/10):** Happens every week.
- **Cool/Unique (3/10):** Standard brewery entertainment.
- **Magnitude (3/10):** Local host, low production stakes.
- **Total:** 7/30

**Event: Morning Sit (Meditation)**

- **Magnitude (1/10):** Peer/Community led.
